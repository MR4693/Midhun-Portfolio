The Engineer

A manufacturing engineer who also
builds the systems the floor runs on.

Every system in this portfolio began as an operational problem I encountered working daily inside a live precision sheet metal manufacturing environment at Ludwick Precision Ltd. Rather than buying commercial software, I specified, designed, integrated and deployed each one against the company's MIE Trak ERP — and I still maintain and improve them today.

The Journey

From paper to a digitised
production environment.

In 2025, core operations were paper-based: job packs assembled by hand, operators clocking on paper, no real-time production visibility, and engineering documents filed manually. The programme below replaced that, one production problem at a time.

2025 · Baseline
Paper-based manufacturing operations
Manual job packs, handwritten clock cards, no real-time production visibility, end-of-shift ERP data entry, manual engineering document filing.
Phase 1 · Deployed
Manufacturing Operations Platform
Android production terminals across the shop floor. Operators clock in, access digital job packs, track live work orders, print move tickets and sign off production — all connected to MIE Trak in real time.
Phase 2 · Deployed
Manufacturing Intelligence Platform
Real-time management dashboards aggregating live production data from 19 operators and 14 work centres — continuous visibility without ERP access or floor walks.
Phase 3 · Deployed
Digital Job Pack Platform
Automated job pack generation — Excel input triggers an ERP lookup; correct engineering documents retrieved and assembled into a print-ready PDF in seconds.
Phase 4 · Deployed
Document Intelligence Platform
Automated engineering document management — drag-and-drop intake with automatic part number detection, folder organisation and MIE Trak ERP attachment.
How It Was Built

AI accelerated the build.
The engineering judgement is mine.

These systems were implemented using AI-assisted engineering, with an AI model used as a software development partner under full engineering direction and accountability. That distinction matters in an interview, so it is stated plainly here.

  • Manufacturing requirements were defined by me, from direct operational involvement.
  • Production workflows and business logic were designed by me.
  • ERP integration was specified and validated by me.
  • Testing and validation remained my responsibility on the live floor.
  • AI accelerated software development — it did not replace engineering judgement.

The result is manufacturing software built by someone who encounters the operational problems it solves every working day — and who remains accountable for maintaining every system.
