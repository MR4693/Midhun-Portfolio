Why It Matters

Visibility is the prerequisite
for every good decision.

Before this platform, the state of production was effectively invisible until end-of-shift. A supervisor who cannot see an overdue work order cannot reschedule around it; a director who cannot see operator efficiency cannot act on it. The cost of that blindness is measured in delayed jobs and decisions made too late. This is the business intelligence layer that removes it.

The Source of Truth

Every figure on every dashboard is drawn live from the MIE Trak SQL Server database — the same data that drives production operations. Data is read on every page load, so the management view is never a stale snapshot. The platform is read-only against the database: zero risk of production data being altered through a dashboard.

Four Dashboard Views

Each view answers one question

The platform is not a single wall of numbers. Each of its four views is built around a specific question a manager needs answered during the working day — and the decision that answer enables.

01 · Main Dashboard

“What is the state of production right now?”

A live production summary with open and overdue work orders at a glance. It is the first screen a director opens — the answer to whether the floor is on track without walking onto it.

Why it matters: overdue work orders previously needed specialist ERP access to find. Surfaced here continuously, scheduling problems are seen as they develop — not after they have caused a delay.

02 · Live Shop Floor

“Who is working on what, and for how long?”

Every active operator session, with elapsed time, the job and the work centre. The entire shop floor — all 19 operators and 14 work centres — in a single live view.

Why it matters: it replaces the floor walk and the phone call. Supervisor time previously spent establishing job progress is freed for higher-value production management.

intelligence.local · Live Shop Floor
03 · Employee Efficiency

“How does actual time compare to estimated?”

Actual versus estimated time per operator, at session level. Performance becomes visible during the shift rather than reconstructed afterwards from manual records.

Why it matters: efficiency data available in real time lets management make informed workload and scheduling decisions during the shift — and feeds more accurate estimating over time.

intelligence.local · Employee Efficiency
04 · Production Insights

“Where is the load, and what are the patterns?”

Work centre load, job distribution and production patterns — aggregated from live operator session records, for supervisors and production planning.

Why it matters: it moves the conversation from “what happened today” to “where do we consistently bottleneck” — the analytics that inform planning, not just reaction.

Operational KPIs

The metrics that drive the floor

The platform surfaces the production indicators that matter to operational decision making — each tied directly to live ERP data, each acted on within the working day.

Work Order Status

Open and overdue work orders, surfaced continuously. The leading indicator of whether the schedule will hold — and the trigger for rescheduling before a delay lands.

Active Operator Sessions

Live clock-on state across all 19 operators with elapsed time, job and work centre — the real-time picture of where labour is being spent right now.

Operator Efficiency

Actual vs. estimated time at session level — the measure of how production reality compares to the plan, available during the shift not after it.

Work Centre Load

Distribution of jobs across all 14 work centres — the view that reveals bottlenecks and informs how work is balanced across the floor.

Production Patterns

Trends aggregated from live session records — the analytics layer that turns daily activity into planning insight over time.

Real-Time Freshness

Every metric drawn live on page load from the MIE Trak SQL Server database — no batch lag between the floor and the management view.

How It Was Built

A delivered system, accountable end to end

The Manufacturing Intelligence Platform is live and in daily use by directors and supervisors. It was implemented using AI-assisted engineering, with an AI model used as a software development partner under full engineering direction — the manufacturing logic, the dashboard design, the SQL against the live MIE Trak database, and the validation were mine.

Engineering Judgement Remains Mine

AI accelerated development; it did not replace engineering judgement. The manufacturing workflows, the system architecture, the business logic, the SQL reporting against the live ERP, and accountability for every figure on every dashboard remain with the engineer who operates the environment.

Operational Impact

What changed on the floor

Continuous Visibility
  • Directors and supervisors see a live view of shop floor activity at any moment — without ERP access, a floor walk, or a call to an operator.
  • All 19 operators and 14 work centres in a single management view, in real time.
Faster, Better Decisions
  • Overdue work orders flagged continuously — scheduling issues acted on as they develop.
  • Operator efficiency visible during the shift — informed workload decisions, not retrospective ones.
Time Recovered
  • Supervisor time previously spent on status floor walks and phone calls returned to higher-value production management.
  • No specialist ERP knowledge required to read production status.
Zero Added Cost
  • Deployed on existing factory infrastructure — no new hardware, licensing or third-party software.
  • Operator data from the Operations Platform feeds the dashboards directly — no duplicate integration.
